Conventional Turbocharger Assembly

A detailed conventional turbocharger assembly designed to simulate the core principles of forced induction. It showcases how exhaust gas kinetic energy is recovered by a turbine wheel and instantly transferred across a balanced shaft to power the intake air compressor, all supported by a standard Center Housing Rotating Assembly (CHRA).

Objective

To design a detailed automotive turbocharger assembly that demonstrates how exhaust gas energy drives a centrifugal compressor to increase intake air density, improving engine volumetric efficiency and performance

Tech Stack

Fusion 360

Turbomachinery Design · Cross-Sectional Assembly · Fluid Dynamics Simulation

Key Learnings

  • Compressor & turbine wheel blade profiling
  • Volute and housing cross-section geometry
  • Concentric component stack-up and mechanical fastener integration
  • High-speed rotordynamic shaft constraints

Technical Implementation

The assembly utilizes a half cutaway cross-section strategy to showcase structural alignment. The core challenges involved managing the aerodynamic curves of the wheels and maintaining concentricity across the high-speed shaft assembly.

Compressor & Turbine Volutes

Modeled via spiral sweeps with variable cross-sections to mimic functional fluid dynamics within the intake and exhaust scrolls.

Central Housing Rotating Assembly

The Center Housing Rotating Assembly (CHRA) was designed to support the common shaft, bearings, and rotating components while maintaining precise alignment and reliable operation between the turbine and compressor.

Mechanical Fasteners & Interfaces

Incorporated localized backplates, precise bolt-hole patterns, and a rugged 6-bolt circular structural interface flange.

The turbocharger assembly was modeled by integrating the centrifugal compressor, radial inflow turbine, Center Housing Rotating Assembly (CHRA), and volute housings into a compact mechanical system that accurately represents the conversion of exhaust gas energy into compressed intake air for automotive forced induction.

Engineering Insight

This project demonstrates the engineering principles behind turbocharger design, highlighting the integration of the turbine, compressor, and rotating assembly. It emphasizes the importance of aerodynamic efficiency, precise shaft alignment, and thermal management in achieving reliable high-speed operation and improved engine performance.

Key Design Considerations:

  • Turbine Wheel Diameter: 58mm
  • Compressor Wheel Diameter: 62mm
  • Impeller blade clearances & profiles
  • Volute variable area-to-radius ratio (A/R)
  • Flange transition and stress points
  • Concentric shaft and rotor alignment
