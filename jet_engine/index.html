Jet Engine Assembly

A detailed conceptual jet engine CAD assembly showcasing the internal turbomachinery architecture including compressor stages, combustion section, turbine assembly, and exhaust system with a partially exposed casing for internal visualization.

Objective

To design and visualize a conceptual jet engine assembly with realistic turbomachinery layout and exposed internal architecture for engineering presentation and motion visualization.

Tech Stack

Fusion 360

Assembly Design · Surface Modeling · Turbomachinery Visualization

Key Learnings

  • Multi-stage compressor and turbine assembly
  • Aerospace-inspired CAD surfacing techniques
  • Internal component packaging and alignment

Technical Implementation

The engine assembly was developed using parametric CAD techniques with carefully aligned rotating stages and internal flow architecture. The partially transparent casing was intentionally designed to expose the compressor and turbine sections for educational and presentation purposes.

Compressor Section

Multiple axial compressor stages were modeled to simulate progressive air compression through rotating blade assemblies.

Turbine & Shaft System

Turbine stages were aligned concentrically with the main shaft to maintain realistic rotational geometry and internal packaging.

Transparent Cutaway Design

The exposed outer casing improves visibility of internal components while preserving the aerodynamic engine silhouette.

"This assembly focuses on the structural arrangement and visualization of jet engine internals, combining mechanical detailing with aerospace-inspired styling."

Engineering Insight

This project explores the internal configuration of a turbine engine and demonstrates how compressor stages, combustion chambers, and turbine blades are integrated within a compact aerospace propulsion architecture.

Key Design Considerations

  • Rotor-stator alignment
  • Blade stage spacing
  • Aerodynamic outer casing profile
  • Concentric shaft assembly management
