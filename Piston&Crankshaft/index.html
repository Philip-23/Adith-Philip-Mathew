Crankshaft & Piston Assembly

A 4-cylinder slider-crank mechanism designed to simulate motion conversion from reciprocating piston movement to rotational crankshaft output using precise CAD constraints.

Objective

To model and simulate a realistic multi-cylinder engine mechanism.

Tech Stack

Fusion 360

Mechanism: Slider-Crank

Key Learnings

  • Kinematic motion control
  • Assembly constraint management
  • Mechanical synchronization

Technical Implementation

The mechanism was built using precise constrained joints to replicate real-world kinematic behavior accurately.

Revolute Joints

Applied at crankshaft connections to allow smooth rotational motion.

Slider Joints

Used to stringently restrict piston movement along a single linear axis inside the cylinder block.

Constraint Logic

Ensured 1:1 synchronized motion between crankshaft throw and connecting rod actuation.

"Driving the crankshaft results in accurate reciprocating piston motion, demonstrating realistic mechanical behavior suitable for further combustion analysis."

Engineering Insight

This model demonstrates the fundamental conversion of linear motion into rotational motion using a slider-crank mechanism. The crankshaft rotation safely drives piston movement through connecting rods, replicating internal combustion engine behavior.

Key Design Considerations:

  • Alignment of piston axes
  • Crank phasing consistency
  • Joint constraints & DOF management
