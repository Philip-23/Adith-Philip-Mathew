Automotive Engine CAD Project

2-Stroke Petrol Engine Assembly

A detailed CAD model of a single-cylinder air-cooled 2-stroke petrol engine featuring a 59 mm bore and 42 mm stroke. The assembly includes the piston, cylinder, crankshaft, connecting rod, crankcase, cooling fins, spark plug, intake port, transfer ports, exhaust port, and flywheel. The model was developed to visualize the internal architecture and operating mechanism of a crankcase-scavenged 2-stroke engine.

Objective

To design and visualize a realistic single-cylinder 2-stroke petrol engine assembly using parametric CAD techniques while demonstrating piston motion, crankshaft operation, and port-controlled gas exchange.

Key Learnings

  • Air-cooled cylinder and cooling fin design
  • Crankshaft and connecting rod assembly modeling
  • 2-stroke intake, transfer and exhaust port layout
  • Piston motion and crank mechanism visualization
  • Mechanical assembly constraints and alignment

Technical Implementation

The engine assembly was developed using parametric CAD techniques. The model incorporates a cylinder bore of 59 mm and a piston stroke of 42 mm. The crankshaft was designed with a crank radius of 21 mm to achieve the specified stroke. Internal components were assembled concentrically to ensure realistic mechanical motion and proper alignment of the piston, connecting rod, and crankshaft.

Cylinder and Piston Assembly

The air-cooled cylinder features external cooling fins and houses a 59 mm diameter piston. Piston ring grooves and wrist pin provisions were included to improve realism.

Crankshaft and Connecting Rod

The crankshaft converts reciprocating piston motion into rotary motion. A 21 mm crank throw was used to achieve the required 42 mm stroke length.

Port Timing Architecture

Intake, transfer, and exhaust ports were incorporated into the cylinder geometry to represent the gas exchange process characteristic of crankcase-scavenged 2-stroke engines.

"This project demonstrates the structural layout and operating mechanism of a single-cylinder 2-stroke petrol engine through a detailed CAD assembly and visualization approach."

Engineering Insight

This project explores the design and operation of a small air-cooled 2-stroke petrol engine. Particular attention was given to crankshaft alignment, piston travel, cooling fin geometry, flywheel design, and port positioning to produce a realistic conceptual model suitable for educational and engineering presentation purposes.

Key Design Considerations

  • 59 mm cylinder bore
  • 42 mm piston stroke
  • Transfer and exhaust port positioning
  • Crankshaft balance and alignment
  • Cooling fin spacing and airflow
  • Spark plug placement
  • Flywheel integration
  • Crankcase volume optimization
